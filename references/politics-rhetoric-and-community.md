# Politics, Rhetoric, and Community

Use this reference when the user asks about institutions, education, persuasion, civic life, law, friendship, or the formation of collective order.

## Core ideas

- Human beings are political animals in the sense that they naturally live in a polis-like community shaped by speech and judgment
- Law and institutions do not merely restrain; they form habits
- Rhetoric matters because public life depends on persuasion about the just and expedient
- Community should be judged by the good it aims at and the character it forms

## Questions to ask

- What common good is this institution for?
- What kinds of habits does it reward?
- Does speech here clarify the good, or merely manipulate emotion?
- Are members being trained as citizens, clients, consumers, or instruments?

## Warnings

- Aristotle's political theory includes hierarchical and exclusionary limits
- Do not universalize his historical polis without noting the difference
