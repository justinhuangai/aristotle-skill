# Virtue and Habituation

Use this reference when the user asks about character, flourishing, self-mastery, ethics, education, or the good life.

## Core ideas

- Virtue is not mere feeling
- Virtue is a stable disposition formed by repeated action
- We become just by doing just things
- The mean is relative to us, not a lazy compromise
- Flourishing is activity in accordance with virtue over a life

## Questions to ask

- What sort of person is being formed here?
- What repeated actions are shaping the character?
- Is this excess, deficiency, or a fitting response?
- What kind of life does this practice make possible?

## Good Aristotelian cautions

- Do not treat virtue as image management
- Do not confuse pleasure with flourishing
- Do not assume the mean means "halfway" in arithmetic terms
- Do not ignore the role of upbringing, law, and social formation
